Nuclear Applications in Medicine

Another important area for the program is on the exploration of the application of nuclear engineering in the medical setting, especially by harnessing its inherent property: radiation. Radiation has found many important applications in modern medicine. For example, x-ray imaging is the most frequently used medical imaging modality and a number of other important imaging technologies such as computed tomography and mammography are also based on x-ray radiation. Although the use of the radiation technology for medical treatments and diagnostics is steadily increasing over the years in Hong Kong owing to advances in medicine and an aging population in our society, there is a lack of fundamental research and professional education in training up sufficient medical radiation professionals to meet the current and future manpower demands.

We will develop novel medical technologies based on lasers. Lasers are now frequently used to perform surgery. One of the main challenges in any surgical procedure is determining where to cut as normal and diseased tissue is often not easy to distinguish. Lasers can help address this challenge as they can be used to determine tissue disease state. We will combine these two abilities of lasers to develop novel laser surgery systems that also use the laser beam to guide cutting. Specifically, we will develop tissue analysis technologies based on laser irradiation of ambiguous tissue sites. The technologies will measure the tissue’s elemental, molecular, and microscopic composition. The measurements will be based on laser induced breakdown spectroscopy, Raman spectroscopy, and fluorescence spectroscopy, respectively. The information can be used to diagnose disease and distinguish different tissue types. Moreover, we will verify the accuracy of tissue analysis using the technologies from objective 1 on animal models. This will be done by comparing laser measurements with histological evaluation of the tissue. This research direction is well synchronized with the opening of the School of Veterinary Medicine and the Laboratory Animal Research Unit at CityU. The proposed radiation research center will be able to readily collaborate with these new centers. Integrate the technologies from objective 1 into modern laser surgery systems. This will enable real-time guided surgery. This direction will also help the university to increase connections with local hospitals for research and teaching purposes.
