Budget Plan

The funding we required will be strategically used to boost not only research activities but also education platform about nuclear risk and engineering in Hong Kong. In order to educate professional students for future unexpected emergency related to nuclear radiation damage and public safety, some funding will be used to build education infrastructures. Also, external funding will support building research infrastructures to integrate related research activities in the same or different disciplines within CityU and to form an advanced platform to enable international collaboration. More importantly, the buildup of this research and collaboration platform will further improve the education quality and student exchange program.

The external funding sources come from Hong Kong and mainland of China, including government grants (e.g. GRF, CRF, ITF, HRMF and NSFC) and industry funds (e.g. CLP Limited and CGN Corporation). We will attract external funding mainly from local region at the first year and then extend funding application to mainland of China after proper setup of testbed and infrastructures. In order to rapidly prototype and launch early research center generation and seed research activities, internal funding support is strongly needed at the very beginning but will be dramatically reduced within five years.

For the education program, the implementation plan will include two important parts. First, providing practical education enables undergraduates and graduates to greatly improve their profile and fulfill requirements of future job market. We plan to strengthen a close collaboration with companies involving nuclear. Actually, this plan has already attracted significant attention from industry. As a start, CLP Limited has promised a 5-year funding support worthy of HK$10million. Second, in support of the discovery-enriched curriculum the implementation plan will include mechanisms to launch research-coupled educational programs by encouraging students to learn latest technology and do projects in the research center, and create top-level workshops or summer school by collaborating with world-class institutes and universities.

The framework to attract external research funding is based on three pillars, each of which embraces several short and long term goals. First, strong basic research in nuclear risk and engineering is essential, as it provides the ideas and observations from which innovation emerges; basic research is the engine for innovation and the research center provides this engine. Second, there must be an opportunity for strategic cross-disciplinary research as is proposed. Third, research must be translated to address specific market opportunities such that, if successful, it may have commercial impact. In the basic research about nuclear reactor and system safety, we will target on funding from UGC and industry. The proposal submissions will be led by Dr. Ji-Jung Kai. This will result in a few proposals that will be submitted as GRF, CRF and ITF projects. In the cross-disciplinary research, such as materials in extreme environments and nuclear application in medicine, Dr. Zuankai Wang, Dr. Kaili Wang and Dr. Condon Lau will lead the effort for submission of the proposals to RGC, ITC and NSFC.

After the education and research pillars being architected, created and implemented, the proposed centre will have a mature development especially with enough external financial sources to self-sustain its growth beyond the CityU’s support.
