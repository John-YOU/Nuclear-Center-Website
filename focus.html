Focus

Problem Solutions to be Pursued

The key goal for this research initiative is to develop disruptive technologies and methods to enhance the nuclear safety, to foster the student training, professional education and policy making for the Hong Kong society, and to link the regional safety in a global landscape.

Our Research Area

  • Nuclear Safety

  • Materials in Extreme Environments

  • Nuclear Applications in Medicine

  • Nanostructured Materials for Sustainable Development

Impact

With its growing importance in the local, reginal and global security, safety, and sustainability, nuclear engineering has been at the frontier of international research arena. In particular, new opportunities and challenges have emerged towards understanding the complexities in the nuclear engineering with the demanding need for low-cost and green energy. Traditionally, nuclear engineering related research at the CityU is conducted separately in different departments and lacks a high level of cohesion and synergy. Thus, the launching of the proposed interdisciplinary center will enable us to leverage on collective efforts of researcher with diverse expertise and interest to develop solutions to a wide range of problems in nuclear engineering. Four new cross-cutting research thrusts have been identified which will enlarge the NRE program common areas and enable more collaborations with others. The aforementioned four thrusts are: (1) Nuclear Safety; (2) Materials in Extreme Environments; (3) Nuclear Applications in Medicine; (4) Nanostructured Materials for Sustainable Development. We seek to make breakthroughs in certain niche areas and the mechanistic insights and knowledge obtained through the joint project will bridge the gap between the nuclear engineering and other disciplines, as well as stimulate new thinking on the social recognition and policy making. By addressing the safety and sustainability challenges through innovative and cooperative research, the results will have significant impact on enhancing the reputation and global outreach of CityU; creating new intellectual knowledge and technological breakthroughs for academia-industry collaboration and economic development; providing a unique hub for the student training and professional education to increase the technological, socio-economic, environmental dimensions of nuclear engineering; and improving the quality of human life.

Transformational Nature of the Work

  1. The proposed Center for Advanced Nuclear Safety and Sustainable Development has a critical mass with diverse and strong academic records to increase the global reach and reputation of CityU. The center will also have participation by multiple organizations in the Mainland China and other countries, which include the Tsing Hua University (Peking), Massachusetts Institute of Technology, CLP Power Hong Kong Limited, China General Nuclear Power Corporation, and Tsing Hua University (Taiwan). This provides a unique opportunity for students and faculty members to interact with researchers in other organizations to address the most important questions.
  2. The scientific and technological advances gained from this center will have the potential to produce remarkable tangible benefits to the regional society. By harnessing the government-industry (CLP)-academia collaboration, we are poised to explore the new model for translating and streamlining scientific findings to tangible benefits. The center will actively seek industrial partners to commercialize the technologies developed in the center.
  3. As the sole Nuclear Engineering program in Hong Kong, the proposed center will support Hong Kong Government with our professional advice regarding to nuclear policies, emergency management procedures, regulation, and severe accident simulation. Currently we are working with Hong Kong observatory to build a simultaneous radiation dispersion system, in response for any possible accident happening to one of the reactors around Hong Kong. The faculty involved in the proposed center will be encouraged to visit central and local government agencies and industries to present the results of this effort. A proper budget has been allocated to support this activity. We will hold seminars together with Hong Kong Society for the Study of Nuclear Radiation (HKSSNR) at least four times per year. These seminars and activities can promote public awareness and understanding to nuclear power as well as increasing the CityU credibility in local Hong Kong society.
  4. During the period of the first three years of the center development, we will work to obtain the opportunity to organize major international conferences in Hong Kong. We expect to attract hundreds of people from all over the world to attend conferences. We will also invite the leaders in the related research area, central and local government organizations, and industrial leaders to attend the conferences. The conference attendees will have opportunities to tour the facilities of the proposed center. The results of this effort will be disseminated in the conferences.
