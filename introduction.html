Introduction

Vision

A multidisciplinary research team with synergetic strengths, consisting of member from nuclear engineering, mechanical engineering, materials science, system engineering, medical physics, biomedical engineering, and creative multimedia, proposes to create a new cross-disciplinary research center (tentatively named as Centre for Advanced Nuclear Safety and Sustainable Development, CANSD) at the City University of Hong Kong (CityU), with the goal to develop and disseminate new knowledge, methods, and technology to the areas of advanced nuclear safety, probability risk assessment, low carbon energy and medical physics as well as to support the student training, public education and policy debates for Hong Kong society.

Nuclear power is a major, sustainable and efficient low carbon emission primary energy source for this century. Today, 25% of the Hong Kong’s electricity comes from nuclear power, which is 12 times of the national average and higher than the share of the nuclear power in USA. During the past two decades, the use of nuclear energy eliminates million tons of CO2 emission (each 1000 MW reactor reduces 6 million tons of emission of CO2/year) and other air pollutions (Sulphur Dioxide, Nitrogen Oxides and Particulates) in Hong Kong. Whether Hong Kong people like it or not, the nuclear electricity will remain to be a major energy in the energy mix of the Hong Kong area and Pearl Delta Region. However, nuclear accidents such as Chernobyl in Ukraine, Three Mile Island in USA and very recently Fukushima in Japan remind us that safety issue of nuclear power is a major concern and that must be taken care of with the utmost care and rigor while we enjoy a lower carbon-footprint lifestyle and better air quality in Hong Kong. In fact, with the rapid development of the nuclear energy in Guangdong, Hong Kong is now located right at the center of one of the highest concentrations of nuclear plants in the world (Figure 1). After two decades operation, the aging and the reliability of the first major Chinese commercial nuclear power plant in Daya Bay is becoming a cause of concern. The consequence to Hong Kong of a major nuclear accident would be unthinkable. Even in the case of a minor incident, not only the living quality of the surrounding citizens will be drastically affected, key economic sectors of Hong Kong such as sensitive Real Estate values will also suffer. The safe operation and the enhancement of reliability in nuclear power plants is a worldwide “grand challenge” and this is particularly significant when the city’s urban areas are located less than 50 km from the currently largest operating nuclear plants in mainland China. The unrelenting increase in the demand for non-renewable fossil fuels has created both environmental and economic problems that seriously upset the sustainability of the World’s continued development. Following the Fukushima accident and consequent pause in approvals for new plants, the new target for China is 58 GWe by 2020, then 150 GWe by 2030 and much more by 2050. However, in the vital area of theory and modeling, which has grown to become a crucial component of fusion and fission technology in the international scene, China has yet to develop the necessary strength.

There is a general consensus today that, as industry plans to continue to evolve on high burn-up, nuclear fuel safety research needs to be maintained, even if fuel research has been performed since the beginning of the nuclear power age. New breakthroughs through interdisciplinary collaborations of experts and the integration of nuclear engineering (neutronics, reactor engineering), mechanical engineering (heat transfer, thermal fluid mechanics and reactor safety), materials science (thermal fatigue, corrosion, irradiation damage), system engineering (risk assessment and reliability), medical physics and biomedical engineering (health physics and radiation protection) and simulation of the plant reliability using stochastic approach, advanced simulation and experimental tools for nuclear materials evolution (Molecular Dynamics, phase field simulation) are needed. Due to the worldwide unique combination of the very high population density, high volatility of the economic activities (banking, real estate, and tourism) and high concentration of nuclear plants, the social economic impacts should be studied in different aspects with a breakthrough approach.

In conjunction with the CLP Low Carbon Energy Education Center (which is donated and sponsored by the CLP Power Hong Kong Ltd.), we will also serve as a unique public knowledge hub for our society to be a platform to educate general public and K-12 students. It will also create a new learning and training environment and experience to our students in CityU, especially for the NRE students, to have access to new ideas and technologies.

There are four major research thrusts in the proposed center which include (1) Nuclear Safety; (2) Materials in Extreme Environments; (3) Nuclear Applications in Medicine; (4) Nanostructured Materials for Sustainable Development.

Summary

The results obtained from this effort will put the City University of Hong Kong at the forefront of the advanced nuclear safety technology and to be the leader in the great China area. This center will provide strong support for development of the expertise in nuclear safety and sustainable development for HKSAR, to provide technical leadership in energy and non-energy applications of nuclear technology, and to serve as a platform to enhance international collaborations. In particular, the benefits to the CityU include (1) setup a world-class research center as an international platform in developing advance nuclear safety technology and risk assessment and to provide technical leadership in energy and non-energy applications of Nuclear Technology in the great China area; (2) support the social and economic advancement of Hong Kong and Pearl Delta Region through the creation of sustainable high level R&D activities in Hong Kong with national and international impact and visibility; (3) be the unique public platform for our society to educate general public, inform public debates on nuclear safety, the impact to our society, and the sustainable development of Hong Kong.
